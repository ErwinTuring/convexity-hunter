# Project Philosophy

Convexity Hunter is a research assistant for identifying assets that deserve further investigation as potential positive-convexity opportunities. It is not an autonomous trading bot, and it does not predict market direction.

## Convexity before prediction

The project looks for payoff structures rather than directional forecasts. A potential opportunity must have downside that is both bounded and bearable. Its payoff should improve nonlinearly as the relevant market movement becomes larger.

Positive convexity alone is not enough. The system must distinguish positive convexity from **cheap positive convexity**: an attractive payoff shape can still be a poor opportunity when its price, carrying cost, or other constraints are unfavorable.

## Hypotheses need evidence

World events and narratives generate hypotheses. They help explain what could change, why an asset might be exposed, and where nonlinear outcomes might appear. Narratives are context, not proof.

Market data supplies evidence. A candidate deserves further investigation only when observable market evidence supports examining the hypothesis and its payoff structure. Evidence may weaken or invalidate a compelling story.

## Honest research outputs

The system should avoid false precision. It should present the relevant evidence, uncertainties, downside conditions, nonlinear-payoff reasoning, and investigation priority instead of unsupported numerical scores.

The final decision belongs to a human researcher. Convexity Hunter organizes inquiry; it does not provide certainty, execute trades, or replace independent judgment.
